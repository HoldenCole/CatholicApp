Advent

Adventus Domini
✦
Summary

Advent is the liturgical season of preparation for the Nativity of Our Lord Jesus Christ. It begins four Sundays before Christmas and ends at the Vigil of Christmas. It is a time of joyful expectation, penitential preparation, and spiritual renewal.

History

The observance of Advent dates to at least the fourth century in the Western Church. Originally a period of fasting comparable to Lent, the Council of Tours (567) prescribed a fast three times weekly from the feast of St. Martin (November 11) to Christmas.

Practice

The faithful are encouraged to prepare through increased prayer, fasting, almsgiving, and attendance at the Rorate Masses celebrated in the early morning by candlelight. The Advent wreath, with its four candles, marks the passing weeks.

Liturgical Notes

Violet vestments are worn. The Gloria is omitted. The organ is silent except to sustain singing. The third Sunday, Gaudete, permits rose vestments and the organ, signifying joy at the approaching feast.

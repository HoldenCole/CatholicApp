Practices of the Saints

Choose a saint whose rule of life will be your own.
Each day, follow their practices and pray with them.
P
St. Padre Pio
Mystic, Stigmatist, Confessor
A life of suffering united to Christ's Passion, intense prayer, and tireless service in the confessional.
Sept. 23 · 1887–1968 · Eight practices daily
T
St. Thérèse of Lisieux
Doctor of the Church · The Little Flower
The "Little Way" — doing small things with great love, total confidence in God's mercy.
Oct. 1 · 1873–1897 · Eight practices daily
T
St. Thomas Aquinas
Doctor of the Church · Doctor Angelicus
The union of prayer and study — seeking God through the intellect illumined by faith.
Jan. 28 · 1225–1274 · Eight practices daily
B
St. Benedict of Nursia
Father of Western Monasticism
Ora et Labora — a balanced life of prayer, work, and holy reading.
July 11 · 480–547 · Nine practices daily
T
St. Teresa of Ávila
Doctor of the Church · Mystic, Reformer
Deep interior prayer toward divine union, joined to practical common sense.
Oct. 15 · 1515–1582 · Eight practices daily
J

Sanctification of ordinary work and daily life — finding God in the middle of the world.

Gentle devotion for laypeople — holiness through sweetness, patience, and love.

St. Padre Pio

Mystic, Stigmatist, Confessor
0  of  8  ·  0%
Verbum Hodiernum  ·  Today's Word
Pray, hope, and don't worry. Worry is useless. God is merciful and will hear your prayer.
— St. Padre Pio
Mane
Morning
Morning Offering
Upon waking, immediately offer your day to God. "Lord, I offer Thee all my prayers, works, joys, and sufferings of this day."
Attend Holy Mass
Padre Pio's Mass was the centre of his life, often lasting two hours. Attend Mass daily if possible.
Mental Prayer (30 minutes)
Spend half an hour in silent meditation upon the Passion of Our Lord.
Per Diem
Throughout the Day
Pray the Full Rosary
Padre Pio called it "the weapon." Pray at least five decades each day.
Spiritual Reading
Read fifteen minutes from the Imitation of Christ, the lives of the saints, or St. John of the Cross.
Visit to the Blessed Sacrament
Sit or kneel before Jesus in the tabernacle, in silence.
Vésperæ
Evening
Examination of Conscience
Before sleep, examine the day's faults and offer them to God's mercy.
Pænitentiæ
Penances
Offer Daily Sufferings
Unite every pain and difficulty to the Passion of Christ — "Co-redemption."

Practices of the Saints

Choose a saint whose rule of life will be your own.
Each day, follow their practices and pray with them.
P
St. Padre Pio
Mystic, Stigmatist, Confessor
A life of suffering united to Christ's Passion, intense prayer, and tireless service in the confessional.
Sept. 23 · 1887–1968 · Eight practices daily
Sequor
T
St. Thérèse of Lisieux
Doctor of the Church · The Little Flower
The "Little Way" — doing small things with great love, total confidence in God's mercy.
Oct. 1 · 1873–1897 · Seven practices daily
Sequor
T
St. Thomas Aquinas
Doctor of the Church · Doctor Angelicus
The union of prayer and study — seeking God through the intellect illumined by faith.
Jan. 28 · 1225–1274 · Eight practices daily
Sequor
B
St. Benedict of Nursia
Father of Western Monasticism
Ora et Labora — a balanced life of prayer, work, and holy reading.
July 11 · 480–547 · Nine practices daily
Sequor
T
St. Teresa of Ávila
Doctor of the Church · Mystic, Reformer
Deep interior prayer toward divine union, joined to practical common sense.
Oct. 15 · 1515–1582 · Eight practices daily
Sequor
J
St. Josemaría Escrivá
Founder of Opus Dei
Sanctification of ordinary work and daily life — finding God in the middle of the world.
June 26 · 1902–1975 · Ten practices daily
Sequor
F
St. Francis de Sales
Doctor of the Church · Bishop of Geneva
Gentle devotion for laypeople — holiness through sweetness, patience, and love.
Jan. 24 · 1567–1622 · Eight practices daily

Ecclesiastical Vowels

De Vocalibus Ecclesiasticis
✦
The pronunciation of Latin used in the Roman Church differs from the Classical pronunciation taught in universities. Ecclesiastical Latin is the living tongue of the liturgy — it is how the saints prayed, and how the Mass is sung today.
The Five Vowels

A — always "ah" as in father, never "ay" as in fate.
E — always "eh" as in bed, never "ee" as in me.
I — always "ee" as in machine, never "eye".
O — always "oh" as in note, never "uh".
U — always "oo" as in food, never "yoo".

Nota Bene  ·  Take Note
The key rule: Latin vowels are pure. English speakers diphthongise (glide between sounds) — in Latin, hold each vowel steady. "Amen" is AH-men, not AY-men.

Summarium  ·  Summary

Latin vowels are pure and stable: A = ah, E = eh, I = ee, O = oh, U = oo. Never diphthongise. Practice with common prayer words until the sounds feel natural.

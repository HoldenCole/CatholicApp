Guide to Confession

De Sacramento Pænitentiæ
✠
Ante Confessionem
Before Confession
i.
Examine Your Conscience
Recall your sins since your last confession. Use the examination below, going through each commandment quietly and honestly.
ii.
Make an Act of Contrition
Be truly sorry for having offended God — not merely from fear of punishment, but from love of Him who is all good and deserving of all your love.
iii.
Resolve to Amend Your Life
Firmly resolve to avoid sin and the near occasions of sin. Without this firm purpose of amendment, absolution cannot be validly received.
✠
In Confessione
During Confession
iv.
Approach the Confessional
Kneel and make the Sign of the Cross. Begin with the words:
℣. Bless me, Father, for I have sinned. It has been [time] since my last confession. These are my sins:
℟. Confess your sins simply, clearly, and completely. State the kind and number of each mortal sin. You may also confess venial sins.
v.
Listen to the Priest's Counsel
The priest may offer guidance and assign a penance. Listen attentively — he speaks in the person of Christ.
vi.
Pray the Act of Contrition
℣. O my God, I am heartily sorry for having offended Thee, and I detest all my sins because I dread the loss of Heaven and the pains of Hell; but most of all because they offend Thee, my God, Who art all good and deserving of all my love. I firmly resolve, with the help of Thy grace, to confess my sins, to do penance, and to amend my life. Amen.
vii.
Receive Absolution
The priest extends his hand and pronounces the words of absolution:
℟. Ego te absólvo a peccátis tuis, in nómine Patris, et Fílii, ✠ et Spíritus Sancti. Amen.
℟. I absolve thee from thy sins, in the name of the Father, and of the Son, ✠ and of the Holy Ghost. Amen.
✠
Post Confessionem
After Confession
viii.
Perform Your Penance
Complete the penance assigned by the priest as soon as possible — it is part of the sacrament.
ix.
Give Thanks to God
Spend a moment in thanksgiving. Your soul has been washed clean in the Precious Blood of Christ.
x.
Avoid the Occasions of Sin
Guard the grace you have received. Flee from persons, places, and things that lead you into temptation.
✠
Examen Conscientiæ
Examination of Conscience
I.
I am the Lord thy God
Non habébis deos aliénos coram me
›
  • Have I doubted or denied the truths of the faith?
  • Have I put my trust in superstition, fortune-telling, or the occult?
  • Have I received Holy Communion in a state of mortal sin?
  • Have I neglected my daily prayers?
II.
Thou shalt not take the name of the Lord in vain
Non assúmes nomen Dómini Dei tui in vanum
›
  • Have I used God's name irreverently or in anger?
  • Have I cursed or used profane language?
  • Have I broken a vow or oath made to God?
III.
Remember to keep holy the Lord's Day
Meménto ut diem sábbati sanctifíces
›
  • Have I missed Mass on Sundays or Holy Days without just cause?
  • Have I done unnecessary servile work on Sundays?
  • Have I been inattentive or irreverent at Mass?
IV.
Honour thy father and thy mother
Honóra patrem tuum et matrem tuam
›
  • Have I been disobedient or disrespectful to my parents?
  • Have I neglected my duties to my family?
  • Have I failed to care for aged or infirm parents?
V.
Thou shalt not kill
Non occídes
›
  • Have I borne hatred, anger, or resentment toward anyone?
  • Have I refused to forgive?
  • Have I harmed another by word or deed?
  • Have I given scandal or led others into sin?
VI.
Thou shalt not commit adultery
Non mœcháberis
›
  • Have I entertained impure thoughts willingly?
  • Have I committed impure acts, alone or with others?
  • Have I been unfaithful to my state in life?
VII.
Thou shalt not steal
Non furtum fácies
›
  • Have I taken what does not belong to me?
  • Have I damaged another's property?
  • Have I failed to make restitution for theft?
  • Have I been dishonest in business?
VIII.
Thou shalt not bear false witness
Non loquéris contra próximum tuum falsum testimónium
›
  • Have I lied or deceived others?
  • Have I engaged in gossip, detraction, or calumny?
  • Have I rashly judged others?
IX.
Thou shalt not covet thy neighbour's wife
Non concupísces uxórem próximi tui
›
  • Have I wilfully entertained impure desires toward another?
  • Have I failed to guard my eyes, imagination, or reading?
X.
Thou shalt not covet thy neighbour's goods
Non concupísces rem próximi tui
›
  • Have I been envious of what others possess?
  • Have I been greedy or excessively attached to material things?

Præparatio Profundior
✠
Quinque Gradus
Five Steps of Preparation
I.
Let your love arise from meditating on the sufferings of Jesus.
II.
See that He suffered because of us, and have true contrition because of Jesus, not because of ourselves.
III.
Cultivate true humility, acknowledging: "I deserve suffering and punishment."
IV.
Behold His Most Precious Blood, and let it kindle a supernatural love, a longing to share in His suffering.
V.
Be sorry not only for your own sins, but also for the sins of all people against God.
Oratio S. Catharinæ  ·  A Prayer of St. Catherine
Holy Spirit, come into my heart,
by your power I journey to You, God,
and grant me charity with fear.

Protect me, O Christ, from every evil thought;
warm me with your sweet love,
so that each burden seems light to me.

My holy Father and my sweet Lord,
help me always in all my endeavours.
Christ love, Christ love.
Composed by St. Catherine of Siena
at Rocca d'Orcia, 1377
after miraculously having learned to write

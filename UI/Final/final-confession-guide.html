Guide to Confession
De Sacramento Pænitentiæ
✟
The Sacrament of Penance is one of the greatest gifts Christ gave to His Church. Your sins are truly forgiven through the ministry of the priest.
Before Confession
Ante Confessionem
Examine Your Conscience
Go through each of the Ten Commandments honestly. Note mortal sins in kind and number. Use the Examination of Conscience in this app.
Make an Act of Contrition
Genuine sorrow for offending God — not just fear of punishment. Perfect contrition (love of God) can forgive even mortal sins if you intend to confess them.
Resolve to Amend
A sincere intention to avoid these sins and the occasions that lead to them. You must intend to try — certainty of success is not required.
During Confession
In Confessione
Begin
Make the Sign of the Cross and say:
"Bless me, Father, for I have sinned. It has been [time] since my last Confession. These are my sins:"
Confess Your Sins
Simply, honestly, completely. For mortal sins, state kind and number. The priest is bound by absolute secrecy — he can never reveal your sins. When finished: "For these and all my sins, I am heartily sorry."
Pray the Act of Contrition
The priest will ask you to pray it. Tap below for the text:
Act of Contrition
"O my God, I am heartily sorry for having offended Thee, and I detest all my sins because I dread the loss of heaven and the pains of hell; but most of all because they offend Thee, my God, Who art all good and deserving of all my love. I firmly resolve, with the help of Thy grace, to confess my sins, to do penance, and to amend my life. Amen."
Receive Absolution
The priest prays the words of absolution. At this moment, your sins are truly, completely, and eternally forgiven.
After Confession
Post Confessionem
Perform Your Penance
Complete it immediately if possible, while still in the church.
Give Thanks
Spend a few minutes thanking God. Your soul is clean.
Avoid Occasions of Sin
Identify and avoid the people, places, and habits that lead you to sin.

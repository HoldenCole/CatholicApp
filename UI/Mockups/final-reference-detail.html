The Holy Rosary
Sacratissimum Rosarium
The most powerful prayer after the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass.
History
Historia
The Rosary as we know it developed over centuries. Tradition attributes its origin to St. Dominic in the 13th century, who received it from Our Lady during the Albigensian heresy. The 150 Hail Marys mirror the 150 Psalms, making it the "Psalter of the laity." Pope St. Pius V standardized the form after the Battle of Lepanto in 1571, when the Christian fleet's victory over the Ottoman navy was attributed to the Rosary. He established the Feast of Our Lady of the Rosary (October 7) in thanksgiving. Every pope since has strongly promoted this devotion.
What It Is
Explicatio
The Rosary is a meditative prayer combining vocal prayer (Our Fathers, Hail Marys, Glory Bes) with meditation on the mysteries of Christ's life. It is divided into three sets of five decades: the Joyful, Sorrowful, and Glorious Mysteries. The traditional practice uses only these three sets — the Luminous Mysteries were added in 2002 and are not part of the traditional form. Each decade consists of one Our Father, ten Hail Marys, and one Glory Be, followed by the Fatima Prayer.
How to Practice
Praxis
Traditionally, specific mystery sets are assigned to each day of the week: Joyful on Monday and Thursday, Sorrowful on Tuesday and Friday, and Glorious on Wednesday, Sunday, and Saturday. The Rosary should be prayed slowly and devoutly, with genuine meditation on each mystery. Our Lady of Fatima requested the daily Rosary for the conversion of sinners and world peace.

Church Teaching
Magisterium
Pope Leo XIII issued eleven encyclicals on the Rosary. Pope Pius XI called it "the compendium of the entire Gospel." Our Lady of Fatima (1917) urgently requested its daily recitation.

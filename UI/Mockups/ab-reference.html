Full entry for "The Holy Rosary"
The Holy Rosary
Sacratissimum Rosarium
The most powerful prayer after the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass.
History
Historia
The Rosary as we know it developed over centuries. Tradition attributes its origin to St. Dominic in the 13th century, who received it from Our Lady during the Albigensian heresy. The 150 Hail Marys mirror the 150 Psalms, making it the 'Psalter of the laity.'
What It Is
Explicatio
The Rosary is a meditative prayer combining vocal prayer with meditation on the mysteries of Christ's life. It is divided into three sets of five decades: the Joyful, Sorrowful, and Glorious Mysteries. The traditional practice uses only these three sets.
How to Practice
Praxis
Traditionally, specific mystery sets are assigned to each day. The Rosary should be prayed slowly and devoutly, with genuine meditation on each mystery. Our Lady of Fatima requested the daily Rosary for the conversion of sinners.
Scripture
Sacra Scriptura
Lk 1:28
Lk 1:42
Mt 6:9-13
Church Teaching
Magisterium
Pope Leo XIII issued eleven encyclicals on the Rosary. Pope Pius XI called it 'the compendium of the entire Gospel.' Our Lady of Fatima urgently requested its daily recitation.
